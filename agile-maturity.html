// Meet the team where it is

How lean is the team really — and where can people grow?

Before prioritizing projects, the framework reads how the Agile team is actually structured — which roles are doubled up, which functions are thinly covered, and where someone is holding a seat they were never equipped for.

Not to grade anyone. To prioritize realistically and give people a path to gain the technical footing they're missing — so a scrum master covering product, or a director without a Gantt chart, gets scaffolding instead of silence.

The point isn't to expose a thin team — it's to run one well. The framework meets the org where it is, keeps people out of decisions they're not equipped for, and turns the gaps into growth paths. Lean teams don't need more process. They need the right person on the right question, and a way to learn the rest.
